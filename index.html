Who We Are

Built by People Who’ve Lived It

Release is a nationally recognized behavioral healthcare community offering safe, private, and confidential support for substance use and mental health across New York and Texas. Founded by Zac Clark, a leader with lived experience in long-term recovery, the organization is committed to breaking the stigma surrounding addiction and mental health nationwide.

Since our start in 2017, our outcomes speak for themselves. We’ve witnessed hundreds of stories of recovery. We know this work changes lives, not just for the people we treat but the communities that surround them. Our team is a strong mix of licensed clinicians, experienced operators and individuals who have gone through their own struggles and come out on the other side.  

We combine clinical expertise with real-world understanding, strong community, and an environment built for sustainable change. Whether you’re seeking support for yourself or someone you love, you’ll find a team that listens, understands, and shows up.

Partial Hospitalization Program (PHP)

Our Partial Hospitalization Program (PHP) provides intensive, structured treatment, creating a balanced and flexible environment for those on their path to recovery. This program is ideal for clients who need more support than outpatient services can offer, but do not require 24/7 residential care.

Intensive Outpatient Program (IOP)

Our Intensive Outpatient Program provides structured clinical care in a flexible format. The 12-week curriculum covers emotional regulation, self-awareness, relapse prevention, boundaries, and other essential tools for recovery and daily life.

Outpatient Program (OP)

Our outpatient program is a step down from IOP. It includes fewer hours but maintains key weekly touchpoints like individual therapy, group therapy, medication management, and family support. This option is best for clients who are ready for more independence while staying connected to care.

Admissions Process

Taking the First Step Toward Care

Our team is largely home-grown, and most of us bring lived experience, giving us a deep understanding of the urgency that comes when someone is ready to seek help. From the first call, you are treated as a client in our care, and we begin supporting you immediately through a consultative, coaching-informed approach. We listen, assess, and help guide you toward the appropriate level of care — whether with us or an outside provider. While we move quickly, our admissions process is also thorough, with our team serving as your advocate and care coordinator every step of the way.

Initial Contact & Screening

Once you reach out to our Admissions team, they will guide you through the process by conducting a screening and gathering essential background information.

Insurance Verification & Clinical Assessment

Our team assesses the appropriate level of care, recommends the best course of action, and verifies your insurance benefits to determine coverage for our clinical services.*

*Please note that our transitional living program nationwide is not covered by insurance and requires private pay.

Placement to Appropriate Level of Care

Once eligibility is confirmed and the appropriate level of care is established, our team will assist you in scheduling your intake and coordinating admission. If we are not the right fit for you, we are committed to helping you find a program or level of care where you can receive the support you deserve.

Case Management

Every plan is built around the unique needs, goals, and lifestyle of each client. Our Admissions Team works closely with clients and their families to develop a personalized Case Management plan that provides the right balance of structure, accountability, and flexibility — whether that means daily check-ins, weekly meetings, or a customized combination of support services.
